Playoff Context

Pete Maravich: Made only 4 playoff appearances in 10 seasons, never advancing past the conference semifinals. His teams (primarily expansion-era Jazz) simply weren't built for deep playoff runs. Maravich — Context & Era-Adjusted Notes

Pace Adjustment: The average NBA pace in Maravich's prime (1970s) was ~106 possessions per 48 minutes, vs ~97 in Irving's era. Maravich's scoring would likely translate to ~27–28 PPG in the modern era when adjusted for pace.

No 3-Point Line: Maravich played his entire prime without a 3-point line. Many of his long-range jumpers came from what would today be 3-point territory. Had the line existed, his scoring averages would almost certainly be higher.

Knee Injuries: Maravich suffered a devastating knee injury in 1978 that effectively ended his prime. He was never the same player after, averaging just 10.1 PPG in his final Celtics season. Without the injury, his career totals would be significantly higher.

📊 Cross-Era Context — The Numbers in Perspective

Key Similarities

  • Assist Numbers: Virtually identical APG (5.12 vs 5.14). Both were score-first guards who could also facilitate.
  • Physical Profile: Nearly identical height/weight — Maravich at 6'5"/197 lbs, Irving at 6'2"/195 lbs.
  • 50 & 60 Point Games: Both have exactly 6 fifty-point games and 1 sixty-point game.
  • Handles: Both are universally considered to have the most creative and effective ball-handling in their respective eras.
  • Playmaking Peak: Both have a career high of 18 assists in a single game.
  • 1st Round Draft Pedigree: Both were top-3 picks (Irving #1, Maravich #3).

Key Differences

  • Volume Scoring: Maravich averaged 24.0 PPG (career) and 30.3 PPG (peak) vs Irving's 21.7 and 26.9. Maravich had a 31.8% rate of 30+ point games vs Irving's 20.2%.
  • Three-Point Shooting: Irving (39.4% on 5,542 attempts) is one of the elite 3PT shooters of all time. Maravich played without a 3-point line for most of his career.
  • Defensive Impact: Irving (1.22 SPG, 0.42 BPG) significantly outpaces Maravich, though era context matters (steals/blocks weren't tracked in Maravich's early years).
  • Team Success: Irving's teams won 55.7% of games vs Maravich's 44.4%. Irving is an NBA champion (2016).
  • Playoff Experience: Irving has 4x as many playoff games (103 vs 26) and significantly better playoff production.
  • Efficiency: Irving's True Shooting (.578) and eFG% (.535) are far superior, reflecting the modern emphasis on efficiency and 3-point volume.
  • Peak Brilliance: Maravich's 68-point game (without 3-point line) and 30.3 PPG season represent a higher raw scoring peak.
  • Longevity: Irving has already played ~300 more games and counting, surpassing Maravich in every cumulative stat category.

College & Pre-NBA Legacy

Pete Maravich — LSU: The most dominant college scorer in history. Averaged an NCAA-record 44.2 PPG over his 3-year varsity career — a record that still stands and will likely never be broken. Scored 3,667 points in 83 games without a 3-point line or freshman eligibility. His flair, passing, and showmanship revolutionized how the guard position was played.

Kyrie Irving — Duke: Played only 11 college games due to a toe injury, averaging 17.5 PPG on .529/.462/.901 shooting splits. Despite the extremely limited sample, his skill level was undeniable and he was selected #1 overall.
